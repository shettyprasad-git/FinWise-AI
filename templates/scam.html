Stay Protected

Scam Awareness

Know the tricks fraudsters use so you never fall victim to them. Knowledge is your strongest shield.

Remember: Your bank or any legitimate organisation will never ask for your OTP, PIN, CVV, or full card number over the phone or via a link. When in doubt — hang up and call back on the official number.
Very Common
OTP Scam

Fraudsters pose as bank employees, TRAI officers, or delivery agents and trick you into revealing your One-Time Password (OTP) to "verify" your account or reverse a failed transaction.

Red Flags
  • Unsolicited call claiming your SIM / account will be blocked
  • Caller asks you to "share" or "read out" the OTP you just received
  • Urgency or threatening language
How to Stay Safe
  • Never share an OTP with anyone — ever
  • Hang up immediately and call the official bank helpline
  • Banks never ask for OTPs over the phone
Rising Threat
QR Code Scam

Scammers send fraudulent QR codes claiming you will "receive" money upon scanning. In reality, scanning initiates a payment from your account — not to it.

Red Flags
  • Someone on OLX / Facebook Marketplace wants to pay via QR code
  • QR code comes with a request to "enter your PIN to receive money"
  • Pressure to scan quickly
How to Stay Safe
  • QR codes are for sending money, not receiving it
  • You never need to enter a PIN to receive a payment
  • Verify any QR transaction before approving
High Risk
Fake Loan Apps

Unofficial lending apps offer instant loans with minimal documentation, then charge hidden fees, access your contacts & gallery, and blackmail you with morphed images if you're late on a payment.

Red Flags
  • App not listed on RBI's authorised NBFC list
  • Requests permission to access contacts, photos, and SMS
  • No physical address or customer care number
How to Stay Safe
  • Borrow only from RBI-registered banks and NBFCs
  • Check the RBI CRILC list before applying
  • Report harassment to cyber police (1930)
Evergreen Threat
Phishing

Fake emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages disguised as legitimate banks, income tax departments, or e-commerce sites to steal your credentials and card details.

Red Flags
  • Misspelled or lookalike domains (e.g. stateb-ank.com)
  • "Your KYC is expired — update now or account blocked"
  • Unexpected prize / refund claim with a link
How to Stay Safe
  • Always check the full URL before entering credentials
  • Go directly to the official website, never via links in messages
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts
Growing Fast
Investment Fraud

Ponzi / pyramid schemes and fake trading platforms promise unrealistic returns (10–30% monthly) to lure victims. Early investors receive payouts funded by newer victims until the scheme collapses.

Red Flags
  • Guaranteed returns that outperform the stock market by far
  • Pressure to recruit others to unlock higher returns
  • No SEBI registration or regulatory oversight
How to Stay Safe
  • Verify SEBI registration at sebi.gov.in
  • If returns sound too good to be true — they are
  • Report to SEBI SCORES portal or call 1800-266-7575
Targeted Attack
Customer Care Scam

Fake customer care numbers appear in Google Search ads above the real ones. When you call, imposters ask for remote access to your device or request payment to "resolve" your issue.

Red Flags
  • Phone number found via a Google ad (not the official site)
  • Agent asks to install AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or similar tools
  • Upfront "processing fee" to resolve a complaint
How to Stay Safe
  • Always get helpline numbers from the back of your card or official website
  • Never install remote-access software for customer support
  • Legitimate support never charges a fee to solve problems
Been Scammed? Act Immediately.

Time is critical. The faster you report, the better chance of recovering funds.
